Why?
Voluntary Sector

1. Volunteering in Wales:

- 938,000 volunteers.

- Contributing ~145 million hours per year.

- Using average hourly wage replacement, valued at ~ **£1.7 billion per year**.

- **Regular volunteers:** 4.3 million contributing 308 million hours.

- **Replacement value:** £3.8 billion (at National Minimum Wage) to £5.6 billion (at 80% median wage).

- **Decline:** 4.7 million to 4.3 million since 2022 | ~22,500 FTE hours.

- **Recovery challenge:** 38% of organisations yet to recover volunteer numbers to pre-pandemic levels.
- Wales disproportionately affected.

- Many organisations in poorest areas (45%) struggled more.

- England and Wales: employs ~ **1.15 million people**, ~3.1% of regional employment.

- Full cost of employees: ~£43.7 billion, ~67% of total organisational expenditure.


2.  Unpaid Carers:


- **Wales:** 370,000+ carers | 96% of all care delivered!

- Saves the Welsh economy ~**£8.1 billion per year**!

- **England and Wales combined:** £162 billion per year (2021), equivalent to a second NHS!

- Equates to **£445 million per day** or £3.1 billion per week.

- Value increased by 29% in real terms since 2011 - fewer carers providing more hours.

- The Carers UK/University of Sheffield: Without unpaid carers, the health and social care system would collapse.

Total UK Voluntary Sector's Income: £69.1bn in 2021-2022 (+9%)

Government Income (Proportion of Total Income): Decline from 30% to 26%
